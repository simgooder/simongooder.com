  • Blueberries need a lot of babying, seem to be a pain in the ass, so we talked about easy to grow blueberry alternatives:
    • Aronia melanocarpa / chokeberry (especially Viking variety)
    • Lonicera caerulea / haskap or honeyberry
    • Amelanchier genus such as Serviceberry or Saskatoon
  • Creeping Charlie / Ground Ivy (Glechoma hederacea) seems to be a common lawn herb, but the bees love it
    • It was used in traditional ale recipes by monks, prior to hops, or when hops weren’t available
  • Speaking of brewing…
    • The Wildcrafting Brewer by Pascal Baudar is filled with very simple/traditional recipes for brewing various concoctions, like country wines, herbal beers, vinegars, etc
    • Sacred and Herbal Healing Beers by Stephen Harrod Buhner is a compendium of truly traditional herbal beer recipes, as they were brewed hundreds of years ago. Lots of good medicinal use-cases and modernized recipes too
  • Trout lilies have edible corms
